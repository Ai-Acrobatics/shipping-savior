## 1. 90-Day Launch Timeline

### Phase Overview

| Phase | Weeks | Milestone |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **Closed Beta** | W1–W4 | 10 design partners onboarded, product feedback loop running |
| **Open Beta** | W5–W8 | 25 beta users active, first paying conversions |
| **Soft Launch** | W9–W12 | Public launch, Product Hunt, first PR hit |

---

### Week-by-Week Breakdown

#### Week 1 — Internal Readiness

**Owner: Founder + Product**

- [ ] Freeze feature scope for beta release (no new features during beta without explicit sign-off)
- [ ] Stand up production environment, verify FTZ Savings Analyzer is stable under load
- [ ] Create beta user onboarding flow: signup → welcome email → 15-min onboarding call invite
- [ ] Draft beta agreement (NDA + feedback commitment: 30-min call + written feedback within 14 days)
- [ ] Build Slack or Discord beta channel for design partners
- [ ] Set up Posthog or Mixpanel: track calculator completions, limit hits, upgrade clicks
- [ ] Create Notion CRM template: Name | Company | Segment | Source | Onboarded | Feedback Status
- [ ] Record 3-minute Loom product demo to attach to outreach

**Deliverable:** Beta environment live, tracking running, outreach materials ready.

---

#### Week 2 — Design Partner Outreach (Freight Brokers + FTZ Heavy Importers)

**Owner: Founder**

- [ ] Identify 40 target design partners from LinkedIn (see beta recruitment section below)

- [ ] Follow up on any warm intros from network
- [ ] Target: 4–6 design partner commitments by end of week
- [ ] Book onboarding calls for committed partners
- [ ] Post in 2 freight brokerage Facebook groups: "Looking for 10 freight brokers to test a new FTZ tool"

**Deliverable:** 4–6 design partners committed, first onboarding calls scheduled.

## 2. Beta User Recruitment Plan

### Goal: 25 Beta Users in 30 Days

**Week 1–2:** 10 design partners (curated, invitation-only)
**Week 3–4:** 10 more via waitlist + open referral
**Week 5:** 5 more via community posts + second outreach wave

---

### Target Beta User Profiles

#### Profile A: Solo Freight Broker (10 targets)
- Licensed freight broker or NVOCC, 1–5 person operation
- Active on LinkedIn, posts about logistics regularly
- Ideal: Has clients importing from SE Asia (Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, China)
- Why they want it: FTZ analysis for client proposals, competitive edge

**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn search: "freight broker" + "NVOCC" + "customs broker" in California, Texas, Florida, New Jersey
- Transportation Intermediaries Association (TIA) member directory
- NCBFAA (National Customs Brokers & Forwarders Association of America) member list
- Facebook Group: "Freight Brokers & Logistics Professionals" (38K members)

#### Profile B: Import Operations Manager (8 targets)
- Mid-size importer, $5M–$50M annual import value
- Industries: consumer goods, electronics, apparel, industrial parts
- Job titles: Import Manager, Supply Chain Director, Trade Compliance Manager
- Why they want it: Quantify FTZ savings without hiring a consultant

**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn search: "import manager" OR "trade compliance" in target industries
- Trade compliance professional groups on LinkedIn
- American Association of Exporters and Importers (AAEI) member list

#### Profile C: FTZ Grantee / Zone Administrator (5 targets)
- Works at a Foreign Trade Zone (there are 700+ active FTZ grantees in the US)
- Motivated to help tenants understand cost savings
- Why they want it: Tool to demonstrate tenant ROI

**Where to find them:**
- NAFTZ (National Association of Foreign Trade Zones) member directory
- LinkedIn search: "FTZ" OR "foreign trade zone" + "administrator"

#### Profile D: Customs Broker (7 targets)
- Licensed customs broker, advises importers on compliance and cost
- Often works alongside freight brokers
- Why they want it: Add FTZ advisory to service offering, generate referral revenue

**Where to find them:**
- NCBFAA directory
- LinkedIn: "licensed customs broker" in major port cities (LA, NY, Miami, Chicago, Seattle)

---

### Recruitment Sequence

**Step 1: Connection request or cold email** (Day 1)
Use LinkedIn connection note or cold email (Script A, Section 8)

**Step 2: Follow-up** (Day 3 if no response)
Short follow-up: "Quick bump — did my note land? Happy to share more context."

**Step 3: Soft close** (Day 7 if no response)
"Closing out my beta invites this week — wanted to give you first access before I open it up."

**Step 4: Onboarding call** (within 2 days of yes)
45-minute call: demo (15 min) + guided use (20 min) + feedback (10 min)

**Step 5: Post-call** (within 24 hours)
Send recap email with access credentials + feedback survey link + Discord invite

Time-limited offer to drive urgency at launch.

- "Founding" language resonates strongly with early adopters in B2B SaaS — use it
- For freight brokers specifically: emphasize ROI in the incentive framing ("this tool helps you win 2 more proposals per month — it pays for itself on day one")
