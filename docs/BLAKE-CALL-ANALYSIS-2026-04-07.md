## Part 2: Product Vision Expansion

### Multi-Modal Transport (NEW)
Blake clarified the product should handle ALL transport modes, not just ocean:
- **Ocean freight** — current focus (Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM schedules)
- **Rail intermodal** — CMA CGM offers ocean-to-rail through Cajon Pass to Salt Lake City
- **Air freight** — airway bills with unique identifiers
- **Drayage** — final mile from port/rail terminal to destination
- **Cross-dock operations** — facility management for transloading

> "Whether it's an airway bill, an ocean bill, a seaway bill, they all have identifying markers specific to each mode of transportation. This could be applicable to all modes."

### Jones Act Carriers (NEW)
Domestic US shipping requires American-flagged vessels:
- **Carriers:** Matson, Pasha Hawaii
- **Routes:** Seattle → Hawaii, Seattle → Alaska, Port Everglades → USVI/Puerto Rico
- **Key difference:** No customs/duties for domestic moves, but still need schedule reliability data
- **Use case:** Julian's client ships steel from Oceanside to Hawaii for luxury home building

> "Just like we can't compare Del Monte to Maersk, we need to signify Jones Act vs non-Jones Act carriers."

### Universal Industry Application
Blake sees the tool working across multiple verticals:
- Cold chain (produce, frozen food, pharma — Blake's expertise)
- Automotive parts
- Personal care products
- E-commerce (potential Alibaba integration for internal use)
- Any containerized cargo

## Part 3: Pricing & Business Model

### Tiered Subscription (Blake's Vision)
1. **Free tier** — Basic planning tools with advertiser support
2. **Premium tier** — Full features, limited users
3. **Enterprise ("Cadillac")** — Unlimited users, custom integrations

### Per-User Bundles
- Small companies: 1 login
- Mid-size: Up to 8 users (trade managers across regions)
- Enterprise: Up to 20+ users

Target customers: NVOCCs (10,000+ globally), freight forwarders, importers/exporters, logistics companies
